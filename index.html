Valued Possessions

What material possessions do you value the most?

Body Part: My Hair

I consider my hair to be the most essential part of my body. It is something that is easily customizable from a straight, prim, and proper girl to a girlboss baddie. You might say this is an opt-out way to avoid selecting something that is essentially a fixed body part such as eyes, nose, or lips—however, as a person who once rocked a half-shaved and half-messy shoulder-length hair due to unwanted circumstances, I consider it to be the most essential part.

And I would like it to grow back on the spot that they had to suction out some liquids...

Clothing: My Oversized Brown Blazer

This brown blazer with shoulder paddings, very formal but when paired with casual outfits it just looks a lot more stylish. Something which catches attention but not necessarily pushing to stand out. Another item of clothing I would select would be my white platform high-cut Converse shoes as these were very trendy when I first bought them and are well-used.

Person: My Family

As I am currently in a strait with the most significant and unbreakable physical bond in my life, I will interpret this question in two ways. First, my family are the most valuable people in my life simply because they are the major providers of my material needs and wants—and at times, it just feels like that. Second, my family ensures I am physically well and safe, showing their unrelenting support and love. But at times, this second interpretation feels like a chokehold where my need to grow, explore, and improve is leashed. And so, it just proves the point that a family bond is so strong that even those who are prideful and unwilling to love back are met with the silent love of care.

Digital Self

Exploring the physical world and the possessions that define my identity.

Describing My Online Presence

As a person born in the age of the Internet, where I experienced several social media such as Facebook, Amino, Tumblr, and more, there are two entities I show depending on who I want to connect with. For platforms such as Facebook and Instagram, I use the real me, use my real name, display myself, and share personal real-life experiences unfiltered. While if I were to interact with anonymous people with the same interest in platforms such as Amino and Twitter, I use a generated codename I have for myself to cover my real entity for privacy and security.

Authenticity Online

I believe my personal social accounts shared with people in close proximity represent my authentic self. I post what I would find to be interesting parts of my life, share music I vibe with, and show my real personality in texts. The only inauthenticity is that I filter what I want others to see such as my hardships, messes, and daily life.

Curation and Filtering

I curate my feed through liking posts which interest me, which is why my algorithm often shows me dances, fashion, cats, and memes which I then share to others in hopes of them receiving the same gratification or interest. I filter my online identity by, once again, showing interesting events in my life such as leisurely activities with family and friends, achievements, good outfits, and such.

Digital Challenges and Pressures

I have three challenges I face. First is the constant comparison to others who often show how good their life is, which is why I constantly have to remind myself that this is just filtered content by the entity I envy. Second is validation; this is apparent in platforms like Reddit where I post opinions in subreddits I would assume have the same bias as me instead of looking for a fair community just for an ego boost. Lastly, is how the digital world has made me so quick to judge people as on the internet, we see, we assume, and do not look for more information on whether that assumption was correct or not.

Guiding Principles

When talking to others on social media, do not say things you wouldn't say out loud in a public area, especially to anonymous users and public chats. The internet is not managed by you; you cannot filter what has already been publicized as people might store it.

Guiding Principles on Bridging the Physical and Digital Self

I believe my true self is already aligned with the activities I do in the digital world. The way to align it more to my true self would be posting more often, which I would rather not do.
