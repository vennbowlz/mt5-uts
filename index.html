Essential Identity: My Hair

I consider my hair to be the most essential part of my physical identity. It is a versatile tool for self-expression, easily transformed from a "prim and proper" look to a "girlboss" aesthetic. While some might view this as an easy choice compared to fixed features like eyes or lips, my perspective is shaped by experience. Having once sported a half-shaved, messy shoulder-length style due to unforeseen circumstances, I realized how much of my agency is tied to it.

My deepest wish is for it to fully recover in the areas where medical intervention was once necessary.

The Power Suit & The Classic

My brown blazer with shoulder pads represents the balance I strike in life: formal and structured, yet effortlessly stylish when paired with casual wear. It commands attention without being ostentatious. Alongside this, my white platform high-cut Converse shoes remain a staple; they represent a blend of trends and reliability, well-worn and well-loved through my daily journey.

The Human Connection

I view the "material" presence of people in two ways. First, my family members are the primary providers of my physical needs; at times, our bond feels defined by that logistical support. However, they also represent the foundation of my safety and well-being. While their protection can sometimes feel like a restrictive leash on my need for independence, it highlights a profound truth: family bonds are so resilient that even in moments of pride or friction, they are sustained by a silent, unrelenting care.

Duality Online

I navigate the internet through two distinct lenses. On Facebook and Instagram, I am my "real" self—using my actual name and sharing unfiltered life experiences with those I know. Conversely, on platforms like Twitter or Amino, I adopt a curated codename. This allows me to engage with shared interests while maintaining a necessary layer of privacy and security.

Digital Pressures

  • Comparison: Reminding myself that I am viewing a "highlight reel," not reality.
  • Validation: Recognizing the temptation to seek ego boosts in echo chambers like Reddit.
  • Judgment: Resisting the internet's tendency to fuel snap judgments based on limited information.

My Golden Rule

"Never post or say anything online that you wouldn't be comfortable saying aloud in a public space. Once something is publicized, you lose the ability to filter or delete it from the digital record."
